THE WORK

“ОЛЬЖИЧ У ЖИТОМИРІ” (“OLZHYCH IN ZHYTOMYR”)

“

Приймав гостей розхристаний Житомир
Так, щоб кати не бачили — вночі.
І там, в якомусь непомітнім домі
Машинка стукотіла при світі.

Якійсь пакети і якійсь папери
Приносили таємні поштарі,
І в домі тім не зачинялись двері
від ранку і до самої зорі.

Й була помітна всім висока постать
І теплий погляд, гострий, як стріла:
- Заходьте, друзі, і вістки приносьте,
тут вистачить для вас усіх тепла.

Тут вистачить для вас усіх роботи
У час, коли здригається земля.
Ви ж бачите, ген біля ешафоту
Зловісний кат з утоми аж закляк.

Багато він був більш,
Ніж сірий і камінний вір.
О.Стефанович

Ах, киньте чвари. Вже від крові сковзько,
А ще ж далеко, друзі, нам іти.
Своєю кров’ю Сеник і Сціборський
до єдності вас кличуть, гей, брати.

Крізь ці пожари і буремні війни
Хіба ж не нам Господь таврує путь?
Соборна. Незалежна. Самостійна -
Ці слова три збагни і не забудь!

... Хто ж він, скажіть, цей лицар полум’яний,
Цей керманич людського почуття,
Що усмішкою гоїть наші рани,
Що мертвих повертає до життя?

Хвала тобі, наш пам’ятний Житомир,
Що його порив ти беріг і лет!
Всім розкажи, що у твоєму домі
Був Ольжич — славен воїн і поет.

”

ABOUT THE LOCATION

ZHYTOMYR REGIONAL UNIVERSAL SCIENTIFIC LIBRARY NAMED AFTER O. OLZHYCH

While walking along Novyi Boulevard, one cannot miss the Zhytomyr Regional Library named after O. Olzhych. Built in Soviet architectural style, the library features a striking mural of Olzhych, unveiled in 2018 on his 111th birthday.

One of Ukraine’s oldest libraries, it opened in the late 19th century with public donations. Initially located in the governor’s house, it became a hub for the “Volhynia Research Society” and welcomed literary figures like Borys Ten and Oleksandr Dovzhenko.

During WWII, the Nazis looted and destroyed much of its collection, but staff managed to save some books by hiding them in the basement. Over the years, the library had several names, finally renamed after Olzhych in 2007.

Today, the library remains a cultural centre, hosting literary events, theatre performances, and even a cosy café where visitors can enjoy books over coffee.

WHO HONOURED IT?

OLEH OLZHYCH (REAL LAST NAME: KANDYBA)

Oleh Olzhych, son of renowned poet Oleksandr Oles, was born in the Zhytomyr region. His family descended from Cossack nobility. Though he spent his childhood in Kyiv, Berlin, and Prague, he often returned to his homeland.

From an early age, he showed literary talent—writing his first play at five. The 1917 Revolution shattered his family's life, forcing his father into exile while Olzhych and his mother struggled to survive. His first published work, Rudko, was a children's story about a rooster, though he later focused on science.

He studied philosophy at Charles University in Prague, became a renowned archaeologist, earned a doctorate, and lectured at Harvard. In 1935, he published his first poetry collection, Rin, followed by Vezhi (1940), infused with historical and patriotic themes.

In 1929, Olzhych joined the OUN, later heading its cultural department. He led the editorial office of Ukrainske Slovo in Zhytomyr before moving to Kyiv as political tensions grew.

Both Soviet and Nazi forces pursued him. In 1944, the Gestapo arrested and brutally tortured him. He died two months before the birth of his son, who was named in his honour.

ABOUT SYTNYK’S POEM

Mykhailo Sytnyk is a Ukrainian poet, editor, and journalist. Together with O. Teliga, he edited the magazine Litavry and was a member of the editorial board of Ukrainian Word (1941). He was an active member of the OUN, a “Melnykite”.

In the poem, “The guests were welcomed by the dishevelled Zhytomyr…” Sytnyk tells about the events of the German occupation of the Zhytomyr region, when the Ukrainian underground functioned on Lermontovskyi Street under the guise of a newspaper editorial office. The poet sincerely admires the figure of Olzhych, who headed Ukrainian Word and worked for the spread of national culture. This poetry is a tribute to the “flaming knight”, who lived and died, relentlessly fighting for a cathedral, independent, independent Ukraine.
