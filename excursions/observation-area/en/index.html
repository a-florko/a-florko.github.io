THE WORK

EXCERPT FROM “ЧОРНА ЗЕМЛЯ” (BLACK LAND)

“

Чепурні будиночки під горою, біля кожного будиночка садок та клапоть городу, кривулясті вулички, порослий травою берег і кам’яниста річечка — все це називається Подолом. Тут же, біля річечки, стримить прямовисна скеля — символ старовинного Подолу, на ній колись височів замок. Зразу ж від скелі простора вулиця пливе між великих будинків, облицьованих керамічними плитками, буяє молодий парк, принаджуючи затишком і прохолодою... проте на Поділ сучасним спорудам не приступитися. В давнину місто виростало саме від цієї скелі: була тут найзручніша позиція для оборони. Ще й досі спускаються на Поділ вулички, де трапляються поруйновані мури, підперті контрфорсами. У нерівному з вибоїнами брукові вгадується хода століть, невидимі сліди багатьох життів переплелися тут. Скільки вже забулося давнього, навіть того, що, здавалося, ніколи не забудеться! Стирається камінь, і нині люди проходять по ньому, не помічаючи нічого особливого. Не привертає уваги й ця хатина з невеликими вікнами та притуленими до стін синіми віконницями, бо вона така ж, як і інші будинки Подолу, хіба що з-за паркану відсвічує на вулицю різнобарвне цвітіння квітів, котрих тут набагато більше, ніж у сусідів...

”

ABOUT THE LOCATION

OBSERVATION DECK

The observation deck is located on Zamkova Hora, offering a stunning view of the Kamianka River and the surrounding private sector. From the site, visitors can also see the Assumption Cathedral. Below the deck, the historical Podil district unfolds, a part of old Zhytomyr stretching across the valleys of the Kamianka and Rudavka rivers.

Podil’s street network and main structures date back to before the 19th century. The area once featured Podilskyi Maidan at the intersection of major roads, and since the 1700s, the Church of the Assumption has stood on Podilska Mountain. In the early 19th century, the first wooden bridge was built over the Kamianka River, later replaced by a reinforced concrete bridge in the 1970s.

THE MAGISTRATE'S BUILDING

The magistrate's building stands on the site of Zhytomyr’s former wooden castle, which burned down in the early 19th century. A Soviet-era structure later replaced it, leaving only the name of the original castle.

Built in the 18th century, the magistrate served as the city hall for only six years before Zhytomyr was annexed by the Russian Empire in 1795. Nearby, visitors can see the ruins of the 1724 Carmelite monastery cells, the oldest preserved buildings in Zhytomyr. The monastery and church, however, did not survive. The magistrate is also located near the Cathedral of the Exaltation of the Cross and a park with a memorial sign marking the city’s foundation.

WHO HONORED IT?

ANATOLIY SHEVCHUK

Born in 1937 in Zhytomyr Oblast, Anatoliy Shevchuk is a Ukrainian writer, former Soviet political prisoner, and member of the National Union of Writers of Ukraine. A supporter of Ukrainian independence, he opposed the suppression of Ukrainian culture.

In 1966, he was arrested for anti-Soviet propaganda after publishing critical articles. Sentenced to five years in strict-regime camps, he joined a nationalist group of dissidents. After his release, he remained under state supervision until Ukraine’s independence. His books could only be published freely after the Soviet Union collapsed.

A Swedish woman, inspired by his writing, sent letters to his prison, but the KGB never delivered them. In the 1990s, he prepared Zhytomyr Sketches on the region’s literary history, but it remained unpublished due to a publishing crisis. A laureate of the Ohienko and Zemlyak literary awards, Shevchuk remains a lesser-known yet influential figure in Ukrainian literature.

ABOUT THE WORK “ЧОРНА ЗЕМЛЯ” (BLACK LAND)

“Black Land” is a story from the book “Zhytomyr Sketches” by Anatoliy Shevchuk, which describes the lives of local residents in the Zhytomyr Podil. For the main characters, it was not just a place where they live but something much more special. So why did the author develop the story line of the work in Podil?

The main character, Maria Stepanivna, recalls significant historical events that took place her: “Here was the most convenient position for defence”. Podil also plays a spiritual role in the work. People considered it as a place where you can distract yourself from all the bad things, and walking there is a salvation from loneliness: “The boulevard was rustling, pigeons were flying; her flowers echoed through the sunny streets from the city to Podil and from afar to her.” Podil is the place where you want to leave a memory of yourself forever: “The flower 'Olya' will live on even after her — this is how Maria Stepanivna will come to people. Even when all memory of her is gone in Podil.”

Of particular importance is the image of the “black land”, a mysterious piece of land that hides the secrets of Podil's inhabitants. “But there is a patch here that everyone calls 'black land'. Whether it was a fire that was here in ancient times or maybe the barn of a wealthy owner, who knows.”

Although all the events unfold in one place, this work carries many hidden meanings, both historical and spiritual.
