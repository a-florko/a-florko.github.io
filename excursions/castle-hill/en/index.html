THE WORK

JÓZEF KRASZEWSKI'S DIARY ENTRIES

“

Кам'янка впадає у Тетерів, річку, береги якої суцільно вкриті скелями і починаючи від гирла її берег тетерівський був чудовим місцем для прогулянки... Скелі тут достатньо високі, місцями оголені, потріскані, осунені, нагромаджені у живописному безладді одна на одну, то сірі, то червоні, то жовті, порослі мохом — все це разом з темною глибиною річки являло собою чудову картину, яку навряд чи зміг би змалювати художник... Береги Тетерева з протилежного боку, починаючи від млина, ближче до води, були засіяні великим камінням, яке безсумнівно упало з гір. Біля самої води росли буйні верби, татарські клени, калина і безліч різноманітних кущів... Для мешканців Житомира ці тетерівські кущі заміняли собою міській сад і служили, особливо для любителів, чудовим місцем приємної прогулянки.

”

This is the only excerpt from our project that is not written in the original in Ukrainian. We used a translation from a book by prominent local historians to honour the writer, archaeologist, and public figure to whom we are grateful for the development of the city's theatre and many artistic and research works.

ABOUT THE LOCATION

ZAMKOVA HORA (CASTLE HILL)

Zhytomyr is a modest yet picturesque city, rich in natural beauty and historical charm. Unlike bustling metropolises, it captivates with its quiet streets, serene parks, and architectural gems like the Transfiguration and Holy Cross Cathedrals. One of the city's most significant spots is Zamkova Hora (Castle Hill), where an ancient fortress once stood. Today, a monument marks the city’s foundation, surrounded by a peaceful park perfect for walks and relaxation.

The true treasure of Zhytomyr lies in its stunning landscapes. As 19th-century writer Józef Ignatius Kraszewski noted, even well-traveled visitors were awed by its wild beauty. The Teteriv River, with its rocky cliffs, lush greenery, and winding banks, offers breathtaking views that have inspired generations. These natural wonders make Zhytomyr a place of quiet charm and enduring appeal.

WHO HONOURED IT?

JÓZEF KRASZEWSKI

Józef Ignacy Kraszewski (1812–1887) was a Polish writer, historian, and publicist, recognized as one of the most prolific authors in European literature. Over his lifetime, he wrote more than 600 works, including historical novels, dramas, and essays, many of which explored Polish history, national identity, and social issues. His novels often depicted the struggles of the Polish people under foreign rule, making him a significant literary and political figure of his time.

Born in Warsaw and educated in Vilnius, Kraszewski was deeply involved in cultural and political life. His literary career flourished alongside his work as a publisher and social activist. In 1853, he moved to Zhytomyr, where he contributed to local education, theatre, and charitable organizations. There, he wrote several notable works and sought to modernize the city's cultural life. However, conflicts with the local nobility led to his departure in 1859.

Later in life, he was imprisoned in Germany for alleged political conspiracy, spending years in exile. Despite this, his influence on Polish and Ukrainian literature remains significant, particularly in the historical genre. His works continue to be studied and appreciated for their historical depth and rich storytelling.

MORE DETAILS

JÓZEF IGNACY KRASZEWSKI’S IMPRESSIONS OF ZHYTOMYR

When Józef Ignacy Kraszewski arrived in Zhytomyr in 1853, he did not find the city appealing at first. In a letter to his brother, he described it as a cultural wasteland, unfit for literature and art. However, over time, his perception changed.

Despite his initial disappointment, Kraszewski actively engaged in the city’s cultural life. He worked to develop education, theatre, and publishing, hoping to modernize Zhytomyr. At the same time, he became captivated by its natural beauty. He admired the landscapes surrounding the Teteriv and Kamianka rivers, the rolling hills, and the serene streets lined with gardens. His later writings described Zhytomyr as a place of harmony between urban and rural life, where houses stood among fields and animals roamed freely.

Zhytomyr also became a source of literary inspiration. Many of his works were written there, including historical novels, dramas, and stories that reflected his deep connection to the region. Although he eventually left due to disagreements with the local elite, his time in Zhytomyr left a lasting impact on both him and the city’s cultural history.
