EXCERPTS FROM “СЛИВОВА КІСТОЧКА” (PLUM PIT)

“

В повітрі літали піщинки. Пустовіт надкуси м’ясисту сливу й відчув хрускіт пилу на губах. Слива була смачною, по підборіддю тік сік, і він витирав його хусточкою. Було жарко, обідня спека загнала людей в будинки. Приблудні собаки висолопили заслинені язики й ховалися в затінку дерев. Обгризену сливову кісточку із залишками м’якоті чоловік кинув у траву, але через кілька кроків вернувся, підібрав патичка, проколупував у землі дірку, запхав кісточку і запечатав землею.

На ратуші пробило чотирнадцяту годину, коли Ігор Пустовіт витер об траву руки й пішов геть, вслухаючись, як подертими афішами шарудить вітер.

****

Біля вежі Ігор побачив велику сливу, на якій висіли жовті плоди. Її трусили галасливі діти, запихаючи в ненажерливі роти. Розчавлені сливи валялися під ногами, і по них лазили великі жирні мухи. “Дивно, – подумав Пустовіт, – ще ж в обід цієї сливи, здається, не було. Я тільки запхав десь тут у землю сливового камінчики”. Розгублено зауважив, що на вулиці Кочерги знесли кілька будинків. “Дивно, – знову подумав він, – а ще ж в обід, здасться, вони були”.

ABOUT THE LOCATION

THE WATER TOWER

In the 19th century, Zhytomyr faced a shortage of quality water, leading to disease and frequent fires. To solve this, a centralized water system was designed by Arnold Jensch and Mieczysław Librovich. Built-in 1898 by a Warsaw-based engineering firm, the 31-meter neo-Gothic Water Tower became a city landmark. Located on Petrivska Hill, it was Zhytomyr’s "skyscraper" and remains a dominant feature of the skyline.

The tower functioned until 1963 when a new pumping station took over. From 1984-1996, part of the building was converted into the Yuvileyne café and a hidden "secret room." Despite modern developments, the tower, sometimes called Zhytomyr’s Pisa Tower, remains an architectural gem.

S. RICHTER ZHYTOMYR PHILHARMONIC

The exquisite S. Richter Zhytomyr Philharmonic, which was officially opened in 1944, is located next to the Tower. In 1858, it was the oldest theatre in Ukraine, which existed for 80 years and changed its status to a philharmonic. It was designed by the famous Ukrainian architect Ivan Strom.

Nowadays, to feel the atmosphere of 19th-century Zhytomyr, visitors can enjoy fragrant coffee in coffee shops or join art events that regularly take place in the Water Tower. Who knows if the plum tree will ever grow there again?

WHO HONOURED IT?

VOLODYMYR DANYLENKO

Born in 1959 in Turovets, Zhytomyr Region. Volodymyr Danylenko is a Ukrainian novelist, essayist, critic, and literary scholar. A laureate of multiple literary prizes, he has authored numerous literary projects.

Coming from a family that opposed Soviet rule, Danylenko developed an early passion for storytelling, realizing at the age of five that he would become a writer. He studied at Zhytomyr Ivan Franko Pedagogical Institute and later defended Ukraine’s first psychoanalytic literary thesis at the Shevchenko Institute of Literature (1994).

Danylenko has curated literary anthologies, notably The Ten-club: Zhytomyr Poetry Gathering (1995), and Dinner for 12 People: Zhytomyr Prose School (1997). A recipient of the Ohienko Award (1999), he aligns his writing style with the aesthetics of Valeriy Shevchuk. His works, known for their humour and depth, appeal to both scholars and general readers.

ABOUT THE WORK “СЛИВОВА КІСТОЧКА” (PLUM PIT)

What does a person leave behind after living a life? Plum Pit is a grotesque-ironic novel, the events of which take place in the heart of Zhytomyr, depicting the life of a person in the city.

The novel is written in the genre of magical realism, bordering on the every day and the fantastic, the real and the imaginary. “Man named after Dnipro” is mentioned in the story. The first meeting with Boris Ten was captured in the author's memory because it turned out to be life-changing. It seems that he felt like a child near Ten. This fascination grew into a passion that led him throughout his life. Danylenko transferred his feelings to paper in the form of Ihor Pustovit's childhood memories. The author describes the poet's “book empire” with special awe.

The main character appears as a person whose personal life is “cheaper than any novel”. The author lays down a philosophy of the shortness of life, symbolised by a plum tree near the Water Tower.
