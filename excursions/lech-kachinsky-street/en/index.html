THE WORK

“ЗОРЯНІ САДИ” (“STELLAR GARDENS”)

“

Висока Піч... Тригір’я... Дениші ...
І небо, і замислена дорога, -
І сосон тихомовні комиші,
І пісня, які ниви ці, розлога.

І відкіля до тих знайомих слів
Щораз нова мелодія незнання?
То ввечері не той почую спів,
Що на моїм шляху стелився зрана.

Не тих почую шепіт верховіть,
Дорожній пил не в тих омию водах
І питиму не тих вітрів я подих.

А вдалині, мов видиво стоїть,
Струнких новобудов піднявши комір,
Століттями не зрушений Житомир.

”

ABOUT THE LOCATION

LEKH KACHYNSKYI STREET

Lekh Kachynskyi Street, named after Poland’s sixth president, extends southwest from Sobornyi Maidan toward Chudniv. The street is historically significant, housing the ruins of a former Jesuit monastery. Founded in the early 18th century, the monastery once occupied a third of medieval Zhytomyr, featuring a church, cells, workshops, and inns.

From the late 19th century to 1941, the monastery’s cells served as a prison where Oleksandr Dovzhenko was once held. During World War II, the site became a ghetto, where Zhytomyr’s Jewish population was confined before mass executions.

Though largely destroyed in wartime bombings, the monastery’s ruins stand as a somber reminder of history, while the surrounding Korbutivka district symbolizes resilience and renewal.

WHO HONORED IT?

BORYS TEN

Borys Ten (Mykola Khomychevskyi) was a poet, translator, and cultural figure. His pseudonym comes from the ancient name of the Dnipro River, Borysfen. Born in 1897 in Volyn, he graduated from the Volyn Institute of Public Education in Zhytomyr and later studied music in Moscow.

In the 1920s, he served as a priest in the Ukrainian Autocephalous Orthodox Church, advocating for Ukrainian culture and language. This led to his exile to the Far East. Though released early, he remained under surveillance.

Borys Ten is best known for his masterful Ukrainian translations of Homer’s Odyssey and Iliad, as well as works by Aristotle, Shakespeare, and Mickiewicz. He also wrote exceptional sonnets, compiled in Stellar Gardens (1970). A talented musician, he contributed to opera translations and folk song collections.

Borys Ten passed away in 1983. His legacy as a literary and cultural figure remains immortal, as poet Dmytro Pavlychko once said.

ABOUT THE WORK “ЗОРЯНІ САДИ” (“STELLAR GARDENS”)

Stellar Gardens is a book of sonnets and the only original work of Borys Ten, a writer from Zhytomyr. Being written for many years, these poems describe different stages of the author's life. His translations of Crimea Sonnets by Adam Mickiewicz, Bloody Sonnets by Pavol Orszagh, and the sonnets of Johannes Robert Becher can also be found here.

It seems reasonable to suggest that the seventeen sonnets from the chapter “Stellar Gardens” are written about the poet's wife Apollinariia Kovalchuk during the time of their separation. These sonnets appear to be filled with their love, mentioning the beautiful voice of Apollinariia and their letters to each other that express the hope that after all the hardships they will be together again.

Borys Ten also mentions native Polissia, often describing the blooming time of the year – spring.

The book also contains sonnets about a range of topics, including human relationships, the Motherland, and renowned Ukrainian writers. It offers insights into the author's experiences upon returning home after the war, the Polissia paths of Zhytomyr, and the time spent on the front and in captivity under fascist rule.
